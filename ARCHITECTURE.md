# Architecture

## System Architecture Overview

This overview is organizational rather than strictly dependency-based: the frameworks remain independently defined but can be grouped by functional layer within the archive.

### Foundational layer

**PFT**, **TFL**, **TCRF**, and **SST** define core perceptual, recursive, temporal, and identity-related processes that are relevant across the archive.

- **PFT** — perceptual framing and interpretive structure  
- **TFL** — recursive simulation, affective persistence, and memory reactivation  
- **TCRF** — temporal coherence, predictive synchronization, and timing stability  
- **SST** — identity stabilization through role-simulation and emotionally weighted feedback  

### Internal systems layer

**IRE**, **VRIF**, and **RIET** model higher-order internal regulation, coherence, and reactivation dynamics that intersect with the foundational layer without being reducible to it.

- **IRE** — internal intention arbitration and action resolution  
- **VRIF** — resonance-based coherence and integration across conscious streams  
- **RIET** — reactivation of identity-echo dynamics under contextual resonance  

### Cross-agent layer

**PCT** occupies the interaction layer of the archive rather than the single-agent internal layer.

- **PCT** — predictive alignment between agents under bounded empirical conditions  

## Interpretive Summary

At a high level, the archive can be read as a layered system:

- the **foundational layer** defines core perceptual, recursive, temporal, and identity-related processes  
- the **internal systems layer** describes higher-order regulation, coherence, and reactivation within a single agent  
- the **cross-agent layer** models predictive alignment between agents once internal processes become structured enough to support anticipatory coordination  

This structure should be read as a functional map of the archive, not as a strict claim that later frameworks are reducible to earlier ones.
